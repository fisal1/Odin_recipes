Biriyani Recipes

Description

Mutton biryani holds a regal legacy from the kitchen of Nawabs and Nizams, differing slightly from region to region in India. This indulgent recipe is a true example of Lucknowi Awadhi culinary heritage.

For Biryani Masala:

  • 2 medium Tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 Green chilies (slit)

  • 3 tbsp Oil
  • 2 tbsp Ghee
  • A few strands of Saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (optional)

Steps to Make Biryani

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    1. Mix all marinade ingredients with chicken.
    2. Let it sit for at least 1 hour (overnight in the fridge for best results).
  2. Prepare Rice:
    1. Boil water with salt and whole spices.
    2. Add soaked rice and cook until it's 70% done (rice should still be firm).
    3. Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook Chicken Masala:
    1. In a heavy-bottomed pan or biryani pot, heat oil and ghee.
    2. Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until tomatoes soften.
    3. Add marinated chicken and cook for 10‚Äì15 minutes until chicken is mostly done.
    4. Add half of the fried onions (birista), mint, and coriander leaves. Mix well.
  4. Layer the Biryani:
    1. Lower the flame. Add a layer of half-cooked rice over the chicken.
    2. Top with remaining fried onions, mint, coriander, and saffron milk (if using).
    3. Optionally, drizzle a little ghee oncd top.
  5. Dum Cooking (Final Steam):
    1. Seal the pot with a tight lid (or dough to trap steam).
    2. Cook on very low flame for 20‚Äì25 minutes.
    3. Alternatively, place the pot over a tawa (griddle) to avoid burning the bottom.
